8th International Agricultural Engineering Conference accepts ICS research papers

 Two ICS research papers have been accepted for oral presentation in the Joint 8th International Agricultural Engineering Conference and Exhibition, 60th Philippine Society of Agricultural Engineers (PSAE) Annual National Convention, and 21st Philippine Agricultural Engineering Week which will be held on 21-23 April 2010 at the Benguet State University (BSU) in La Trinidad, Benguet. The papers are:

  1. Runoff and Flooding Simulation on Selected Philippine Catchment Areas Using Cellular Automata and SRTM by J.P. Pabico and J.A.C. Hermocilla
  2. The Network Structure of Scientific Research Collaboration of Agricultural Engineers in the Philippines by J.P. Pabico

Nine ICS research papers to be presented at the PCSC 2010

Nine (9) ICS research papers have been accepted for presentation in various modes (oral and poster) at the 10th Philippine Computing Science Congress (PCSC 2010). These are:

  1. Automated Vein Density Measurement Using In-Focus Region Isolation by Maria Art Antonette D. Clariño and Vladimir Y. Mariano
  2. Automated Rice Plant Health Assessment System using Digital Image Analysis by Reinald Adrian DL. Pugoy and Vladimir Y. Mariano
  3. Identifying Catchment Areas near Selected Mountains in the Philippines using FlowViz by Joseph Anthony Hermocilla and Jaderick P. Pabico
  4. Interveinal Analysis on Rice Leaf Images by Francis Michael F. Paje, Maria Art Antonette D. Clariño and Vladimir Y. Mariano
  5. When Leechers are left to tend for Themselves: Performance of Orphaned File Sharing Networks by Irish Charisma B. Flores and Jaderick P. Pabico
  6. Design and Performance of a Lightweight Distributed Desktop Web-Grid by Marie Yvette B. de Robles and Jaderick P. Pabico
  7. Simulating the Foraging Behavior of Trigona biroi Using Multi-Agent Systems by Roberto B. Figueroa, Jr., Jomar F. Rabajante and Arian J. Jacildo
  8. Simulation of Infectious Disease Spread Based on the SIR Epidemic Model by Francisco Enrique Vicente G. Castro and Jaime M. Samaniego
  9. Counting Stingless Bees, Trigona biroi, Using Image Analysis by Melanie L. Saldua, Arian J. Jacildo, Vladimir Y. Mariano, Margarita Carmen S. Paterno, Jomar F. Rabajante and Alejandro C. Fajardo, Jr.

Ms. Rosario to present computational statistics approaches on fraud detection

Ms. Geraldine Rosario will present a seminar on fraud detection on Thursday, 18 February 2010. The talk will discuss various computational techniques such as Peer Group Analysis, Anomaly Detection, Time Series Trend Analysis, Text Mining, Social Network Analysis, and many other Machine Learning, Statistical and Data Mining techniques applied by experts in fraud detection. The talk will start at 10:00 a.m. and will be conducted at ICS Mega Lecture Hall.
